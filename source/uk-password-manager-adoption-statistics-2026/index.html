📋 Executive Summary

Password manager adoption in the UK has nearly doubled from 18% in 2020 to 35% in 2025, driven by mainstream data breach coverage, built-in browser password managers in Chrome and Safari, and employer-mandated cyber security training. Despite this growth, nearly two-thirds of UK adults still manage passwords manually, and adoption among younger adults (18-24) lags behind older demographics.

🌱 Key Growth Drivers

The doubling of adoption from 2020 to 2025 is attributed to several factors:

  • Media coverage: Major data breaches (T-Mobile, Optus, Uber, LastPass itself) raised public awareness of credential security.
  • Browser integration: Chrome and Safari now prompt password saving by default, making manager adoption passive rather than active.
  • Passkey transition: Apple and Google's passkey rollout (2023+) introduced users to credential management concepts.
  • Remote work: Hybrid working drove corporate password policy enforcement and manager deployment.
  • NCSC guidance: The NCSC's public advice to "use a password manager" and "don't reuse passwords" reached mainstream audiences.

📋 Research Methodology

Data on this page is compiled from multiple authoritative sources: Ofcom's annual Online Nation report provides the primary UK consumer adoption data. The DCMS Cyber Security Breaches Survey provides UK business data. Growth trends and demographic breakdowns are supplemented by Statista market reports and industry analyses from Keeper Security and NordPass annual studies.

Note: Exact year-on-year figures for 2023-2025 are estimates based on published trend data, as Ofcom and DCMS reports are published with a reporting lag. The 2025 data point is from the most recently published Ofcom Online Nation report.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What percentage of UK adults use a password manager?

According to Ofcom's Online Nation report, approximately 35% of UK adults use a password manager as of 2025. This represents significant growth from 22% in 2022, driven by increased awareness of cyber security risks and built-in browser password managers.

Is password manager adoption growing in the UK?

Yes. Password manager adoption in the UK has grown from an estimated 18% of adults in 2020 to 35% in 2025 — nearly doubling in five years. The growth is attributed to data breach coverage in mainstream media, built-in browser managers (Chrome, Safari, Edge), and employer-mandated cyber security training.

Which age group uses password managers the most in the UK?

Ofcom data shows password manager usage is highest among 35-54 year olds (42%), followed by 55+ (38%) and 25-34 (34%). Surprisingly, 18-24 year olds have the lowest adoption rate at 28%, often preferring to rely on browser autofill or memory despite being the most digitally active demographic.

How many UK businesses use password managers?

The DCMS Cyber Security Breaches Survey 2025 found that 67% of UK businesses have implemented password management policies or tools. Adoption is highest among large businesses (89%) and lowest among micro businesses (48%).

Do free browser password managers count as password manager usage?

Yes. Ofcom's data includes built-in browser password managers (Chrome, Safari, Edge) in its adoption figures. Approximately 60% of password manager users rely on built-in browser tools, while 40% use dedicated third-party managers like LastPass, 1Password, Bitwarden, or Dashlane according to industry estimates.
