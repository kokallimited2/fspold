UK Cyber Breaches Statistics 2026

📅 Updated: 15 July 2026 | 📊 Sources: DCMS, ICO, Home Office, NCSC

Key Takeaways

  • 32% of UK businesses experienced a cyber breach in the last 12 months — rising to 62% for large businesses
  • 84% of breaches involve phishing, making it the most common attack vector by a wide margin
  • Average breach cost ranges from £1,200 (micro) to £18,105 (large businesses)
  • The ICO receives over 3,000 data security incident reports annually, with cyber-related incidents accounting for ~60%
  • UK ransomware payments by businesses reached an estimated £1.2 billion in 2024-25

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most common type of cyber breach in UK businesses?
Phishing is the most commonly reported breach type, affecting 84% of UK businesses that experienced a breach. This includes fraudulent emails designed to trick recipients into revealing credentials or downloading malware.
Which UK sector reports the most data security incidents?
The health sector consistently reports the highest number of data security incidents to the ICO, accounting for approximately 26% of all reports. This is largely attributed to the sector's size and the sensitivity of the data handled.
Are UK businesses getting better at cyber security?
Yes. The overall breach rate has declined from 39% (2022) to 32% (2025), suggesting improvements. Adoption of multi-factor authentication has grown by 22 percentage points and cloud-based security by 18 points. However, the frequency and sophistication of attacks continue to increase.
