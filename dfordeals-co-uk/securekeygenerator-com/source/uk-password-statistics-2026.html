Methodology & Data Notes

Password reuse and behaviour statistics are drawn from NCSC-commissioned YouGov polling (sample: ~4,500 UK adults). Most common passwords data is aggregated from NordPass annual reports and NCSC's own analysis of breached credentials from HaveIBeenPwned. Business breach statistics come from the DCMS Cyber Security Breaches Survey 2025 (sample: 2,200 UK businesses). Cybercrime cost estimates are from the Home Office Crime Survey for England and Wales. Password manager adoption figures are from Ofcom's Online Nation 2025 report. ICO incident figures are from published ICO data security trends reports. All figures are the most recent available as of July 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are password managers safe to use?
Yes. Password managers encrypt your credential database with a master password using AES-256 encryption. They are significantly safer than reusing passwords, writing them down, or using easily guessable patterns. The NCSC recommends password managers as a secure way to maintain unique passwords across all accounts.
How many passwords does the average person have?
The average UK adult has approximately 100 online accounts but uses only 5 unique passwords across all of them. This means each password is reused ~20 times on average.
What makes a password strong?
A strong password contains at least 12 characters, mixes uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols, avoids dictionary words and personal information, and is unique to each account. The NCSC recommends using three random words as a memorable base.
How quickly can a hacker crack a password?
With modern GPU-based cracking hardware, an 8-character password can be cracked in under 8 hours. Most of the top 100 most common passwords are cracked in under one second. A 12-character random password would take centuries to brute-force.
