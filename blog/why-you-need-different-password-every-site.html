🔐 Why You Need a Different Password for Every Website

By Sarah Mitchell · 3 May 2026 · 4 min read

Category: Beginner Guide Author: Sarah Mitchell Published: 3 May 2026 Reading time: 4 min read

In this guide

  1. What is credential stuffing?
  2. The easy fix

Imagine using the same key for your house, your car, your office, and your bank. If someone copied that key once, they'd have access to everything. That's exactly what happens when you reuse passwords online.

What is credential stuffing?

When a website gets hacked, your username and password can end up in a list sold to criminals. These criminals then try your login details automatically on hundreds of other websites — banking, email, shopping. This is called credential stuffing, and it happens millions of times every day.

The easy fix

Use our free password generator to create a unique password for every site, then store them all in a free password manager like Bitwarden. You only need to remember one master password — the manager handles the rest.
