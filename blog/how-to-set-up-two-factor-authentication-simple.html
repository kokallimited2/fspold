📱 Two-Factor Authentication Made Simple: A Step-by-Step Guide

By Sarah Mitchell, Digital Safety Writer, FreeStrongPassword.com · 22 Apr 2026 · 3 min read · 86 words

Two-factor authentication (often called 2FA or two-step verification) adds a second check when you log in — like a text message code or a notification on your phone. Even if someone steals your password, they still can't get in without this second step.

How to turn it on

Most major websites — including Gmail, Facebook, Amazon, and your bank — offer 2FA in their settings. Look for 'Security', 'Privacy', or 'Account settings' and look for 'Two-step verification' or 'Two-factor authentication'. Enable it and follow the steps.
